About

Abdullah Khan

I’m Abdullah Khan, a Computer Science graduate with a Master’s degree from the University of Wah, Pakistan, and over six years of full-stack development experience. My academic and professional journey has been deeply rooted in cutting-edge technologies, particularly at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, cybersecurity, and quantum computing. My research has centered around deepfake audio detection, large language models (LLMs), and the development of quantum-classical hybrid architectures for secure and efficient machine learning applications. I’ve led and contributed to projects ranging from transformer-based zero-shot voice cloning detection to quantum gate optimization for holographic IoT environments, alongside publishing and submitting research in areas such as CAN bus security and quantum neural networks.

Driven by a passion for advancing the frontiers of secure and explainable AI, I am now seeking to pursue a PhD where I can further explore the convergence of deep learning, quantum computing, and real-world security applications. I aspire to contribute to impactful research that enhances trust, transparency, and robustness in AI systems, especially those integrated into sensitive domains like autonomous vehicles and voice-enabled consumer devices. With a strong foundation in both practical development and academic research, I aim to be part of interdisciplinary teams building the next generation of secure, intelligent systems.

Research

What I Do

  • 01

    Quantum-secure voice authentication for IoT environments

    Developed a hybrid quantum-classical system to detect synthetic audio in IoT holographic environments.

    Read More

    In this study, I developed a hybrid quantum-classical system optimized for detecting synthetic audio in resource-constrained IoT environments. Evaluating 28 quantum gate configurations, the CNOT+S combination emerged as the most effective.

    The final QNN model outperformed Deep Learning Models in accuracy with significantly fewer parameters and a lower effective dimension, making it ideal for real-time anomaly detection in holographic systems like smart homes.

    Submitted:
    Abdullah Khan, et al. (2025). Efficient Quantum Neural Networks for Synthetic Voice Identification in IoT-Enabled Holographic Counterparts. Manuscript submitted to IEEE Transactions on Consumer Electronics.

  • 02

    A Dynamic Approach for Detecting Attacks in CAN

    Designed a hybrid framework combining deep learning and rule-based logic to detect anomalies in CAN.

    Read More

    This research combined RNN-based models (LSTM, GRU) with rule-based logic to detect anomalies in Controller Area Networks, which are critical for autonomous vehicle communication. The model accurately identified spoofing, fuzzing, and DoS attacks using time-series sequence modeling and adaptive thresholding, showing strong generalizability and real-time detection capabilities.

    Published:
    Abdullah Khan, et al. (2025). A Dynamic Approach for Detecting Attacks in Controller Area Networks. In Proceedings of the 2025 International Conference on Emerging Technologies in Electronics, Computing, and Communication (ICETECC), Jamshoro, Pakistan, 23–25 April 2025, IEEE, DOI: 10.1109/ICETECC65365.2025.11070255..

  • 03

    Combating misinformation with transformers

    Compiled a dataset of over 40,000 articles and evaluated transformer models for fake news detection.

    Read More

    I curated and processed a large-scale dataset of over 40,000 news articles from open-source platforms for misinformation detection. I then implemented transformer models such as BERT, LLaMA, and RoBERTa for binary classification. The project also included attention-based interpretability analysis, providing insights into how these models differentiate real from fake content.

    In Progress:
    Development of a Large-Scale Fake News Dataset and Transformer-Based Detection Models. Write-up in progress; Aiming for submission.

    Quantum-enhanced medical imaging

    Developed a hybrid quantum-classical convolutional neural network for brain tumor classification from MRI scans.

    Read More

    This project introduced a hybrid quantum-classical CNN model to classify brain tumors from MRI images. By integrating quantum layers into the convolutional architecture, the model achieved competitive accuracy with reduced complexity. Evaluations on the BraTS dataset demonstrated the promise of quantum computing in enhancing the efficiency of medical image analysis.

  • 05

    Survey on Voice Cloning Detection

    Conducted a comprehensive survey on voice cloning detection, identifying limitations in dataset diversity and adversarial robustness.

    Read More

    This project involved an in-depth literature review on voice cloning detection strategies using deep learning and signal processing. It highlights current challenges in consumer-level deployment, dataset limitations, and vulnerabilities to adversarial attacks. The work also outlines future directions such as explainable AI, privacy-preserving detection, and advanced-neural techniques, serving as a roadmap for upcoming research in audio forensics.

    In Progress:

  • 06

    Explainable cybersecurity for automotive systems

    Developed a lightweight framework integrating MLP, GRU, and heuristic rules to detect attacks in CAN.

    Read More

    In this project, I developed a hybrid intrusion detection system that combines a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP), a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU), and domain-specific heuristic rules to detect sophisticated attacks such as replay and flooding. With a detection accuracy of 99.73%, and precision, recall, and F1-score all exceeding 99.7%, the model drastically reduces detection latency to just 54 microseconds per sample, outperforming traditional deep learning approaches.

    It also incorporates SHAP-based explainability to ensure transparent model predictions and is optimized for real-time, resource-constrained vehicular environments, offering a robust and interpretable defense solution for next-gen automotive systems.

    Submitted:
    An Explainable DL-Heuristic Framework for Automotive Attack Detection in Controller Area Networks. Manuscript submitted to Mehran University Research Journal of Engineering and Technology.
